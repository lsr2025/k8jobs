Monday at 11:00 · Mkuze Airport, Mtubatuba
5+ yrs ✓ CIPC ✓ Code 10 ✓ LED ✓
Behavioural Questions
01
Tell us about yourself and why you are applying.
Open with impact, not your CV. "I've spent 5 years on the ground helping entrepreneurs formalise and grow — I want to do that at scale in uMkhanyakude." Lead with the result, not the story.
02
Tell us about a time you helped an SMME access funding.
Have 2–3 real examples ready. Name the programme (SEFA, SEDA, DSBD). Walk through STAR: Situation → Task → Action → Result. Numbers make it real.
03
Describe a moment you managed multiple urgent priorities.
Show a real system — not just "I work well under pressure." Mention weekly planning, stakeholder communication, how you made the call on what to prioritise first.
Technical Questions
01
What experience do you have with CIPC compliance?
Name specifics: new registrations, annual returns, director amendments, B-BBEE affidavits. Practitioners name things — that's what separates a real expert from someone who Googled it.
02
What government funding programmes do you know?
SEDA (non-financial support), SEFA (R50k–R15m loans), NEF (equity), DSBD, dtic, NYDA. Briefly explain each. Don't just list acronyms — explain what each does and who it's for.
03
How would you run a One-Stop Business Support Centre?
This is your strongest question — you've done it. Cover intake, referrals, record management, reporting. Then add: proactive outreach to entrepreneurs who don't know the centre exists.
Situational Questions
01
12 entrepreneurs are waiting, 3 have urgent issues. What do you do?
Triage. Acknowledge everyone first. Handle urgent compliance issues first. Schedule the rest with specific times. Document everything. Show calm under volume.
02
A key funding partner pulls out 2 weeks before a workshop.
Show calm: assess the gap, communicate immediately with all stakeholders, name 2–3 backup partners you'd call. SEDA, NEF, local chamber. Always have a plan B.
03
How would you reach entrepreneurs in rural areas who don't know UMDA exists?
Community radio, taxi ranks, traditional leaders, stokvels, churches, local schools. Show you understand the geography of uMkhanyakude — not just a generic answer.
How to Close the Interview
"What does success look like in the first 90 days in this role?"
Signals leadership thinking — you're already thinking about impact, not just getting the job.
"What are the biggest challenges SMMEs in uMkhanyakude currently face?"
Shows you've thought beyond the job spec. It turns the interview into a conversation.
"Is there anything in my application you'd like me to clarify?"
Opens the door for any concerns — lets you address the degree gap or anything else directly.
Before you leave
Thank each panel member by name. Confirm next steps and timeline. Ask for a business card. Send your referees (Ms. Thobile Nxumalo, Mr. Bahle Magwaza) a heads-up WhatsApp before Monday — they may be called.
